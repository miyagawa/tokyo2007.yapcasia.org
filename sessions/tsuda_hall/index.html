Higher-Order Perl

One of Perl's most valuable (but least-used) features is the ability to use functions as if they were data: to pass functions as arguments to other functions, and to write functions that construct and return new functions on request. This feature is the basis of many widely-used techniques, such as callbacks, and also some techniques that aren't as widely-used, but should be. But what will the talk actually be about? Mr. Dominus has not yet decided. He fervently hopes to have the slides written up by March 1, and will know then.

Way to the NAPC

We don't doubt CPAN is our source of power. We can't imagine Perl without modules.However, when we install or package something, we realize we don't have IT yet. We have a variety of ways to go, but they are more or less winding, obscure, or rugged and most seasoned walkers may slip. Good old CPAN and EUMM, Module::Build and CPANPLUS, Module::Install, PPM, PAR, and various os-dependent package managers including rpm and apt. Given TMTOWTDI, what should we use, and how?As a ppm repository manager who installs and packages everyday, I'll make it a bit clearer what we need, and what we are doing.As always, I'll talk in Japanese, though slides will be written in English.
